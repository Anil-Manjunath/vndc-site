An evocative journey through consciousness and contemporary existence.

About Jaaga Illa

"Jaaga Illa" questions the nature of urban space through the stark confines of a wooden box, mirroring Bangalore's transformation from garden city to technological hub. Within this deliberate containment, bodies negotiate physical and philosophical boundaries, challenging what it means to belong in a city that grows taller but not deeper. The choreography turns restriction into resistance, revealing how urban compression reshapes our ways of moving and connecting. Through movement, dancers embody the tension between expansion and confinement, between vanishing green spaces and gleaming towers, ultimately asking whether a city's growth should be measured by the height of its buildings or the depth of its community's roots.
