About Yele Oota

"Yele Oota" explores humanity's most fundamental connection â€“ our relationship with food and the act of sharing nourishment. In a world of overflowing tables and bare ones, the piece questions how we allowed sustenance to become a commodity rather than a basic right. The dance oscillates between aggressive accumulation and the gentle art of giving, drawing on ancient Indian traditions where feeding others was the highest form of service. Through movement, it celebrates the wisdom of community kitchens and shared meals, building toward a powerful message: generosity is not just a moral choice but a survival strategy for human civilization.
